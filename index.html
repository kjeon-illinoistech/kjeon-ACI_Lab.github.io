About

The Advanced Construction Intelligence (ACI) Lab at Illinois Institute of Technology bridges human expertise with artificial intelligence to revolutionize construction quality management and infrastructure resilience.

We develop AI-driven technologies that integrate domain knowledge with cutting-edge machine learning methods — including Natural Language Processing, Large Language Models, and Knowledge Graphs — to address critical challenges in construction defect management, building lifecycle management, and community resilience.

Housed in the Armour College of Engineering, our interdisciplinary team works at the intersection of civil engineering, computer science, and human-computer interaction to create smarter, more sustainable built environments.

Research

We develop intelligent systems that combine human knowledge and AI to improve construction quality and infrastructure resilience.

Research Areas

AI for Construction Management

Leveraging LLMs, NLP, and Knowledge Graphs for intelligent defect detection, automated information extraction, and construction decision support.

Building Information Modeling

Advancing BIM-integrated technologies for smarter infrastructure design, construction, and lifecycle asset management.

Resilient Infrastructure

Developing predictive maintenance and risk assessment systems to enhance the long-term resilience of civil infrastructure and communities.

Human-AI Collaboration

Designing interactive systems that effectively integrate human domain expertise with AI capabilities for improved decision-making.

Current Projects

Active

PRT Intelligence Dashboard

An interactive analytics platform for Pittsburgh Regional Transit (PRT) rider feedback. The system combines survey data preprocessing, SQLite databases, and an AI-powered route Q&A assistant to surface complaint themes, visualize route performance, and support data-driven transit improvement decisions.

Active

University Facility Maintenance Intelligence (UFMI)

A full-stack predictive maintenance system for university facilities powered by XGBoost and SHAP-based machine learning to predict equipment failure risks. Features include interactive risk heatmaps, defect intelligence via BERTopic text clustering, cost analytics, and an ML explainability dashboard.

Join Our Lab

We are looking for passionate researchers to shape the future of intelligent construction systems.

Ph.D. Students

We seek self-motivated doctoral candidates with backgrounds in civil/construction engineering, computer science, or related fields. Strong interest in AI/ML and construction technology is preferred.

  • Full funding available for qualified candidates
  • Background in NLP, ML, or BIM is a plus
  • Strong research and writing skills required

M.S. Students & Postdocs

M.S. students and postdoctoral researchers with interest in intelligent construction systems, human-AI interaction, or data-driven infrastructure management are welcome to apply.

  • Research and thesis opportunities available
  • Interdisciplinary collaboration encouraged
  • Industry connections and career mentorship

Undergraduate Researchers

Undergraduate students interested in research experience in AI, data science, or construction engineering are encouraged to reach out. No prior research experience required.

  • Hands-on research experience
  • Exposure to cutting-edge AI tools
  • Mentorship from graduate students and faculty
